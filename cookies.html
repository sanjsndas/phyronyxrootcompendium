🍪 COOKIES & EARTHLY TRACES

Just as mycelium leaves invisible threads beneath the forest floor, our digital grove uses small data tokens — called cookies — to enrich your journey through the Root Compendium. These humble fragments help remember your preferences, understand how root-readers wander through our pages, and ensure the soil (your experience) remains fertile. This policy explains what cookies we plant, why we tend them, and how you can manage your own harvest.

🌾 Our cookie philosophy

We believe in transparency akin to clear forest light. Cookies placed on your device by phyronyxcompendium.org are used solely to improve site functionality, analyze anonymous usage patterns, and provide a smooth, earthy experience. We never sell your data or use tracking for exploitative purposes. Our approach mirrors regenerative agriculture: we take only what’s needed and give back through meaningful content.

🔧 Managing cookies via browser

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. To delete or block cookies from Phyronyx Root Compendium, consult your browser’s help section. For example, in Chrome you may navigate to Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data. In Safari, use Preferences → Privacy. Every browser offers a unique path, much like different soil profiles. We recommend keeping essential cookies enabled to enjoy features like contact forms and session stability.

🌻 Why we honor cookie transparency

Just as a forest depends on clear cycles of decomposition and regrowth, the health of the web relies on transparency between sites and visitors. The Phyronyx Root Compendium was born from the idea that digital spaces can embody ecological principles: mutual respect, minimal waste, and regeneration of trust. Our cookie practices reflect that: we gather only what is useful, anonymize analytics to protect your privacy, and never engage in invasive ad tracking. We think of every byte as a seed — planted with intention. By reading this policy, you become part of a movement that values digital ethics as much as soil ethics. Thank you for being a mindful visitor of our rootstead.

🍪 Quick cookie FAQ (Rootlets' queries):
