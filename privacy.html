🌿 1. Information We Collect

a) Personal Data You Provide: When you subscribe to our newsletter, register for workshops, comment on articles, download root guides, or contact us, we may collect your name, email address, general location (region/country), and gardening interests (e.g., permaculture, vegetable gardening, herbalism). We only collect what is necessary to enrich your experience and deliver the wisdom you seek.
b) Automatically Collected Data: Like most websites, we gather certain technical information automatically: IP address, browser type, device identifiers, referring URLs, pages visited, and timestamps. This helps us analyze trends, improve our content, and guard against security threats — all while respecting your anonymity.
c) Cookies & Similar Tech: We use essential cookies to remember your preferences (e.g., dark mode or reading lists) and analytics cookies to understand which articles flourish most. You can manage cookie settings in your browser; however, disabling them may affect certain features like saving favorite plant profiles.

🌱 2. How We Use Your Information

We use collected data solely to:
– Deliver the gardening wisdom you seek, including personalized recommendations and seasonal planting reminders.
– Send newsletters, exclusive content, and updates about the Compendium if you have opted in (every email includes an instant unsubscribe link).
– Improve website performance, troubleshoot errors, and protect against fraudulent activity.
– Respond to inquiries, support requests, or community contributions with warmth and expertise.
– Analyze aggregate usage patterns to expand our compendium in ways most beneficial to our earthkeeping audience.
We never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. Ever. Your data is as precious as mycelium networks.

🍂 3. Legal Basis for Processing (for EEA & global transparency)

If you are in the European Economic Area, our processing of your personal data is based on: your consent (when you subscribe or accept cookies), contractual necessity (if you purchase a product or enroll in a course), or legitimate interests (improving our educational mission and maintaining security). You have the right to withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal. We respect data subject rights under GDPR, UK GDPR, and similar frameworks.

🌾 4. Data Sharing & Third Parties

We may share anonymized or aggregated data with trusted service providers who help operate our website (e.g., hosting platforms, email delivery services like Mailchimp or self-hosted systems). These parties are contractually bound to protect your data and may not use it for any purpose other than providing services to the Compendium. In rare circumstances, we may disclose information if required by law, to protect the safety of our community, or to enforce our Terms of Service. No data brokers, no shady soil.

🌻 5. Your Rights & Control Over Data

Depending on your location (EEA, UK, Switzerland, California, etc.), you may have the right to:
– Access, correct, or delete your personal data.
– Object to processing or request data portability.
– Withdraw consent for marketing communications (every email includes an instant unsubscribe link).
– Opt-out of cookie-based tracking via our cookie banner or browser settings.
To exercise these rights, please contact us at privacy@phyronyxcompendium.org. We will respond within 30 days, nurturing your request as carefully as a graft union. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (e.g., ICO in the UK or CNIL in France).

🪶 6. Data Security & Retention

We implement robust security measures — SSL encryption, firewalls, restricted access, and regular audits — to safeguard your information. No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, but we strive to use best practices and industry standards. We retain personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law. Inactive newsletter subscribers are periodically removed to keep our garden tidy. User contributions (forum comments) remain visible unless deletion is requested, but we anonymize or delete personal data upon request.

🌍 7. Children’s Privacy

The Compendium is intended for a general audience, but we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13. If you believe a child has provided us with data, please contact us, and we will delete it promptly. We encourage parents and guardians to explore gardening together under supervision. Our content is educational and family-friendly, but we respect age-related restrictions.

🌿 8. International Transfers

Our servers are located in the United States. If you access our Site from outside the U.S., please be aware that your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the U.S. where data protection laws may differ. By using our Services, you consent to such transfer. We take steps to ensure adequate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses where required, to protect your privacy across borders.

🍃 9. Cookies & Similar Technologies – Deeper Insight

We use both session cookies (which expire after you close your browser) and persistent cookies (which stay until deleted). You can manage preferences via our cookie consent banner. Essential cookies enable core functionality like navigation and security; analytics cookies (such as those from privacy-respecting, self-hosted analytics) help us understand which root articles are most beloved. We do not use invasive tracking, fingerprinting, or share data with advertising networks. For more details, see our full Cookies Policy or adjust your settings anytime.

We encourage you to review it periodically. Significant changes will be communicated via newsletter or site banner — because informed gardeners are empowered gardeners. Your continued use of the Site after any changes indicates your acceptance of the updated policy.
